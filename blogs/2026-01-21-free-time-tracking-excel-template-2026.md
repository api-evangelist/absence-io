<div class="wp-block-uagb-advanced-heading uagb-block-1ad40207"><h2 class="uagb-heading-text">How the Time Tracking Excel Template Works</h2></div>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading">For Admins</h4>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>The template defines 8 hours of work per day by default. You can adjust this under the &#8220;Working Hours/Day&#8221; cell.</li>



<li>All weekdays (Mon-Fri) are defined as workdays in the template. You can adjust the actual number of workdays per month (affected by holidays, which vary by state, or Saturday work) under the &#8220;Workdays/Month&#8221; cell.</li>
</ul>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading">For Users</h4>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Enter the time you started your workday under &#8220;Start&#8221; (e.g., 09:00).</li>



<li>Enter the time you ended your workday under &#8220;End&#8221; (e.g., 16:00).</li>



<li>If you took a break, enter the duration of your break in minutes under &#8220;Break&#8221; (e.g., 00:30).</li>
</ul>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">General</h3>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Everything is entered in hh:mm format, e.g., 09:00 for 9 AM.</li>



<li>Enter break times in the same format, e.g., 00:30 for a 30-minute break.</li>



<li>The table automatically calculates your hours.</li>



<li>Under &#8220;Balance&#8221;, you can see how many hours you still need to work in the corresponding month. If the number exceeds 0 and reaches the positive area by the end of the month, your overtime will be automatically displayed.</li>
</ul>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading">Vacation</h4>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Enter the number of your vacation days for the respective month under &#8220;Vacation Days&#8221;. The hours will then be deducted from the hours to be worked accordingly. For better clarity, you can color-code your vacation days.</li>
</ul>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Excel Templates: Time-consuming, Error-prone, Insecure</h2>



<p>Keep in mind the following aspects when using an Excel template for time tracking:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Time-consuming creation of the Excel table: Creating it requires time and effort, as formulas and formatting need to be inserted, adding complexity.</li>



<li>High time expenditure for entering working hours: Manual entry can be time-consuming, possibly leading to sporadic updates and inaccuracies.</li>



<li>Inaccuracy in recording working hours: Manual entries increase the risk of errors and inaccuracies.</li>



<li>Error-proneness of Excel: Excel sheets are prone to errors, especially when mishandled.</li>



<li>Lack of transparency: Difficulties in getting a comprehensive overview of all employees&#8217; working hours due to scattered data.</li>
</ul>
